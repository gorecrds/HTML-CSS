Investing Basics

Stock Fundamentals

CONCEPTS:

Ticker

A stock ticker is a unique series of letters assigned to a publicly traded company, used to uniquely identify it on the stock exchange. For example, AAPL is the ticker symbol for Apple Inc.

Value of Stock

The value of a stock represents the price at which a share of the company is currently trading on the stock market, influenced by supply and demand, company performance, and market conditions.

P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings Ratio)

The P/E ratio compares a company's current share price to its per-share earnings, providing insight into stock valuation relative to earnings and helping compare companies within the same industry.

Earnings

The earnings date marks when a company will release its next earnings report, a critical time for investors as it can significantly affect stock prices based on the report's outcomes.

P&L Report (Profit and Loss Report)

The P&L report, or income statement, details a company's revenues, costs, and expenses over a period, offering insights into its operational efficiency and profitability.

Major US Index

Nasdaq:

The Nasdaq Composite is a stock market index that includes almost all stocks listed on the Nasdaq stock exchange. Over the past decade, the index has experienced significant growth, reflecting the robust performance of the technology sector, which constitutes a large portion of the index. Approximately, the Nasdaq has more than doubled in value over the past 10 years, highlighting its substantial growth and the increasing market capitalization of its listed companies.

Dow Jones Industrial Average (Dow):

The Dow Jones Industrial Average is one of the oldest and most well-known stock market indices in the world. It represents the stock performance of 30 large, publicly-owned companies based in the United States. Over the past decade, the Dow has also seen considerable growth, albeit at a slower pace compared to the Nasdaq, reflecting the broader economic and industrial expansion. The index has grown approximately by 70-80% over the last 10 years.

Standard & Poor's 500 (S&P 500):

The S&P 500 is a market-capitalization-weighted index of 500 of the largest publicly traded companies in the U.S. It is widely regarded as the best single gauge of large-cap U.S. equities. Over the last 10 years, the S&P 500 has seen significant growth, around 100-110%, indicative of the overall growth and resilience of the American economy and its leading corporations.
